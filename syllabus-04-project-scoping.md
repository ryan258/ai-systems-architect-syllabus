# Syllabus 04: Project Scoping
**Status:** Learn Next — Unlocks Paid Work
**Format:** One chat session per unit, self-paced

---

## What This Is

Scoping means defining exactly what a project includes before you start. Without it, projects grow without limit and you don't get paid for extra work. This is one of the most important business skills you can learn.

---

## Unit 1 — What Scoping Is and Why It Matters

**Goal:** Understand what scope creep is and why it destroys solo consultant income.

**Topics:**
- What scope creep means (a project quietly growing beyond what was agreed)
- Why clients add requests without meaning harm
- How scoping protects both you and the client

**Session starter:**
> "Explain scope creep to me in plain language. Why does it happen? Give me a concrete example involving an AI workflow consultant. What would have prevented it?"

---

## Unit 2 — The Right Questions to Ask Before Starting

**Goal:** Build a list of questions you ask every client before agreeing to work.

**Topics:**
- What problem are we actually solving?
- What does done look like?
- What is NOT included?
- Who makes decisions if something changes?

**Session starter:**
> "Help me build a discovery question list for AI workflow consulting projects. These are the questions I ask a client before I agree to start work. What must I always ask?"

---

## Unit 3 — Writing a Simple Scope Document

**Goal:** Produce a one-page scope document you can use with real clients.

**Topics:**
- What goes in a scope document
- Plain-language descriptions of deliverables
- What to include in the "not included" section
- How to handle revisions

**Session starter:**
> "Teach me how to write a scope document for an AI workflow consulting project. Give me a simple template I can fill in. Include a section for what is NOT included."

---

## Unit 4 — How to Handle Scope Creep When It Happens

**Goal:** Know what to say when a client adds requests that weren't agreed on.

**Topics:**
- How to say no without damaging the relationship
- How to price additional requests
- Scripts for common situations

**Session starter:**
> "Give me word-for-word scripts for handling scope creep. What do I say when a client asks for something that wasn't in our original agreement? Keep the tone friendly but firm."

---

## Unit 5 — Scoping for Low-Energy Work

**Goal:** Build scope documents that protect you on bad MS days.

**Topics:**
- How to build buffer time into a project without lying about it
- Asynchronous delivery as a scope feature
- How to write a scope that allows for flexible pacing

**Session starter:**
> "Help me write scope documents that build in flexibility for my health without oversharing. How do I include realistic timelines and async delivery as standard features of how I work?"

---

## Practice Project

Write a scope document for a real or imagined project. Include: what you will deliver, what you will not deliver, timeline, and revision policy. Keep it under one page.
