# Syllabus 06: Positioning — Explaining What You Do
**Status:** Learn Next — Unlocks Paid Work
**Format:** One chat session per unit, self-paced

---

## What This Is

Positioning means having one clear sentence that says who you help and what problem you solve. Without it, even great work is hard to sell. Clients buy when they feel understood, not when they're impressed.

---

## Unit 1 — What Positioning Is

**Goal:** Understand why "I do AI workflows" is not enough.

**Topics:**
- The difference between describing what you do vs. who you help
- Why vague positioning loses clients to more specific competitors
- What a strong positioning statement looks like

**Session starter:**
> "Explain what positioning means for a solo consultant. Why is 'I build AI workflows' not enough? Give me examples of weak positioning vs. strong positioning for someone in my field."

---

## Unit 2 — Finding Your Ideal Client

**Goal:** Get specific about who benefits most from your work.

**Topics:**
- Who has the most painful version of the problem you solve?
- Who can afford to pay for the solution?
- Who do you actually enjoy working with?

**Session starter:**
> "Help me figure out who my ideal client is as an AI workflow architect. Ask me questions to help narrow it down. I want to get specific — not just 'small businesses' but a real description of a real type of person."

---

## Unit 3 — Naming the Problem You Solve

**Goal:** Describe the client's pain in their words, not yours.

**Topics:**
- What does your client complain about before they find you?
- What words do they use that you should use too?
- How to research what your market actually says

**Session starter:**
> "Help me figure out how to describe the problem I solve in my client's language, not mine. What questions should I ask? How do I find out what words my potential clients use to describe their pain?"

---

## Unit 4 — Writing Your Positioning Statement

**Goal:** Draft one sentence that you can use everywhere.

**Format:** "I help [who] do [what] so they can [result]."

**Topics:**
- Testing the sentence out loud
- What to do if it sounds too generic
- How to make it specific without making it too narrow

**Session starter:**
> "Help me write my positioning statement as an AI workflow architect. Use the format: I help [who] do [what] so they can [result]. Work through several drafts with me until we get one that sounds true and specific."

---

## Unit 5 — Using Your Positioning Consistently

**Goal:** Put the same clear message in every place a client might find you.

**Topics:**
- Website headline
- GitHub bio
- Email signature
- Verbal introduction

**Session starter:**
> "Help me write my positioning statement in five different formats: a website headline, a GitHub bio, an email signature line, a LinkedIn summary, and a verbal introduction. Same core message, different lengths."

---

## Practice Project

Write your positioning statement. Say it out loud three times. Then send it to one person who knows your work and ask if it sounds true to what you actually do.
