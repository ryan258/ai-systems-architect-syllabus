# Syllabus 01: AI Workflow Design
**Status:** Solid — Keep Sharpening
**Format:** One chat session per unit, self-paced

---

## What This Is

You already do this. This syllabus helps you go deeper, spot edge cases, and explain what you build to clients.

---

## Unit 1 — What Makes a Good System Prompt

**Goal:** Understand why some prompts work reliably and others don't.

**Topics:**
- Role, context, constraints, output format
- The difference between a one-time prompt and a reusable one
- What to always include vs. what to leave flexible

**Session starter:**
> "Teach me what separates a reliable system prompt from a fragile one. Give me a before-and-after example and explain what changed."

---

## Unit 2 — Chaining Prompts Together

**Goal:** Build multi-step workflows where output from one step feeds the next.

**Topics:**
- What prompt chaining means
- How to pass output cleanly between steps
- When chaining breaks and how to catch it

**Session starter:**
> "Teach me how to chain prompts together in a workflow. Show me a simple 3-step example and explain how output flows from one step to the next."

---

## Unit 3 — Building Specialist Personas

**Goal:** Design AI personas that stay in role and produce consistent results.

**Topics:**
- How to define a persona's role, limits, and voice
- Keeping personas from drifting off-task
- When to use one persona vs. many

**Session starter:**
> "Teach me how to design a reliable AI specialist persona. What must I define upfront? What causes personas to drift, and how do I prevent it?"

---

## Unit 4 — Connecting Tools Together

**Goal:** Understand how to link AI with other tools like Notion, Google Docs, or APIs.

**Topics:**
- What MCP (Model Context Protocol — Anthropic's standard for letting AI safely read data from outside tools) does
- How data moves between tools
- What can break and how to plan for it

**Session starter:**
> "Explain how AI connects to external tools in plain language. What is MCP? How does data move between an AI and a tool like Notion or Google Docs?"

---

## Unit 5 — Designing for Low-Energy Days

**Goal:** Build workflows that still work when you are exhausted or foggy.

**Topics:**
- Energy-aware design principles
- Defaults, templates, and decision reduction
- How to test a workflow on a bad day

**Session starter:**
> "Help me design an AI workflow that works even on my worst brain fog days. What principles should guide the design? What makes a workflow fragile vs. robust?"

---

## Unit 6 — Documenting a Workflow So Others Can Use It

**Goal:** Write clear enough documentation that someone else could run your workflow.

**Topics:**
- What to document and what to skip
- Plain-language step descriptions
- How to write a handoff document

**Session starter:**
> "Teach me how to document an AI workflow so that someone else could pick it up and use it. What must I include? Show me a simple template."

---

## Practice Project

Pick one workflow you have already built. Write a one-page plain-English description of it covering: the problem it solves, the steps involved, and what the output looks like.
