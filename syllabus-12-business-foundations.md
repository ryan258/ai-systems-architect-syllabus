# Syllabus 12: Business Foundations
**Status:** Longer Term — Build This Over Time
**Format:** One chat session per unit, self-paced

---

## What This Is

The practical business side of solo consulting. Pricing, contracts, knowing when to say no. You do not need all of this today — but you need it before you scale.

---

## Unit 1 — How to Price Your Work

**Goal:** Understand value-based pricing and why hourly rates often hurt you.

**Topics:**
- Hourly pricing rewards slow work and punishes efficiency
- Value-based pricing charges for the result, not the time
- How to figure out what your work is worth to the client

**Session starter:**
> "Explain value-based pricing in plain language. Why is hourly pricing a trap for solo consultants? How do I figure out what to charge for an AI workflow project? Give me a simple framework."

---

## Unit 2 — Simple Service Packages

**Goal:** Define two or three specific services you offer with clear prices.

**Topics:**
- Why vague services are hard to sell
- How to create a tiered service menu
- What to include in each package description

**Session starter:**
> "Help me define two or three service packages for my AI workflow consulting work. I need clear names, clear descriptions, and suggested price ranges. Help me think through what each package includes."

---

## Unit 3 — Basic Client Contracts

**Goal:** Have a simple contract you can use for every project.

**Topics:**
- What a basic contract must cover
- Payment terms (when and how you get paid)
- What happens if the project changes
- How to make it plain English

**Session starter:**
> "Help me understand what must be in a basic client contract for solo consulting work. I am not a lawyer — give me the five things every contract must cover and explain why each one matters."

---

## Unit 4 — How to Know When to Say No

**Goal:** Recognize the signs of a bad-fit client before you commit.

**Topics:**
- Red flags in early client conversations
- Projects that will drain more than they pay
- How to decline gracefully without burning bridges

**Session starter:**
> "Help me identify the warning signs of a bad-fit client. What should I watch for in early conversations? How do I say no to a project without being rude or burning a relationship?"

---

## Unit 5 — Getting Paid Reliably

**Goal:** Set up payment terms that protect you from late or missing payments.

**Topics:**
- Deposits before starting work
- Milestone payments for longer projects
- What to do if a client does not pay

**Session starter:**
> "Teach me how to set up payment terms that protect me as a solo consultant. How do deposits work? When should I ask for them? What do I say if a client won't pay on time?"

---

## Practice Project

Write your service menu. Two or three offerings, each with a name, a one-sentence description, what is included, and a price range. Keep it to one page.
