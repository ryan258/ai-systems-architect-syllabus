# Syllabus 04: Finishing Systems
**Status:** Learn Next — Unlocks Paid Work
**Format:** One chat session per unit, self-paced

---

## What This Is

A minimum viable deliverable (MVD) is the smallest version of something that still has real value. This syllabus helps you build the habit of finishing — and defining what finished actually means before you start.

---

## Unit 1 — Why Finishing Is Hard

**Goal:** Understand the specific patterns that lead to unfinished projects.

**Topics:**
- Perfectionism as a finishing block
- How energy depletion interrupts completion
- The difference between pausing and abandoning
- How starting feels rewarding but finishing feels harder

**Session starter:**
> "Help me understand why I start projects and don't always finish them. What are the most common psychological and practical reasons? Don't be clinical — be honest and direct."

---

## Unit 2 — What "Done" Actually Means

**Goal:** Learn to define a finish line before you start.

**Topics:**
- Defining done in concrete, observable terms
- The difference between "polished" and "complete"
- How to write a done condition in one sentence

**Session starter:**
> "Teach me how to define what 'done' means for a project before I start it. What questions should I answer? Give me a template for writing a one-sentence done condition."

---

## Unit 3 — What Is a Minimum Viable Deliverable?

**Goal:** Understand how to ship a smaller version that still has real value.

**Topics:**
- MVD means useful, not perfect
- How to cut features without cutting value
- How to communicate an MVD to a client honestly

**Session starter:**
> "Teach me what a minimum viable deliverable is. How is it different from a half-finished project? Give me examples in the context of AI workflow consulting. Help me understand how to use this without feeling like I'm cutting corners."

---

## Unit 4 — Building a Completion Habit

**Goal:** Create a personal system for closing open loops.

**Topics:**
- Weekly review of open projects
- The two-minute rule: if it takes less than two minutes, do it now
- How to decide when to abandon vs. finish vs. pause something

**Session starter:**
> "Help me build a simple weekly habit for closing open project loops. I have a lot of started-but-not-finished things. What's a realistic system for someone with MS and variable energy?"

---

## Unit 5 — Finishing One Real Thing This Week

**Goal:** Apply everything by completing one actual deliverable.

**Topics:**
- How to pick the right thing to finish first
- How to time-box a completion session
- How to ship it even if it isn't perfect

**Session starter:**
> "Help me pick one unfinished project from my work history and define a minimum viable version I could actually finish this week. Ask me what I have. Help me scope it down to something completable."

---

## Practice Project

Pick one unfinished project. Write a one-sentence done condition. Set a two-hour time limit. Finish it.
