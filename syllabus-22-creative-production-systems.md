# Syllabus 22: Creative Production Systems
**Status:** Solid — Keep Sharpening
**Format:** One chat session per unit, self-paced

---

## What This Is

You have built real creative projects. This syllabus helps you systematize your process so you can repeat it faster, hand it off, or sell it as a service.

---

## Unit 1 — Mapping Your Current Production Process

**Goal:** Write down exactly how you move from idea to finished creative project.

**Topics:**
- Identifying your actual steps vs. your assumed steps
- Where you lose momentum
- What decisions you make the same way every time

**Session starter:**
> "Help me map my current creative production process for audio drama. Walk me through the right questions to ask myself so I can identify each step from idea to finished script."

---

## Unit 2 — Building Reusable Templates

**Goal:** Create templates that make starting a new project faster and easier.

**Topics:**
- What belongs in a template vs. what stays custom
- How to write templates that work on low-energy days
- Templates for scripts, song specs, brand guides

**Session starter:**
> "Help me build a reusable template for starting a new KVOID horror script. What should always be pre-filled? What should I fill in fresh each time?"

---

## Unit 3 — Writing Prompts That Produce Consistent Output

**Goal:** Build prompt libraries that give you reliable results from AI music and voice tools.

**Topics:**
- What makes a Suno style prompt repeatable
- How to lock a vocal identity across a multi-track project
- ElevenLabs voice consistency across episodes

**Session starter:**
> "Help me build a reusable prompt library for Suno that produces consistent results across an entire album. What variables should I lock in? What should I adjust per track?"

---

## Unit 4 — Managing a Multi-Part Project

**Goal:** Keep track of a project with many pieces without losing the thread.

**Topics:**
- Simple tracking for multi-episode or multi-track projects
- How to pick up a project after a break without losing context
- What to document as you go

**Session starter:**
> "Help me design a simple tracking system for a 10-episode audio drama project. I need to be able to pick it up after a bad MS week and know exactly where I left off."

---

## Unit 5 — Turning Your Process Into a Sellable Service

**Goal:** Package your creative production knowledge as something a client could hire you to do.

**Topics:**
- What clients might pay for in this space
- How to describe the service without overwhelming them
- What a simple service offer looks like

**Session starter:**
> "Help me figure out if my audio drama or AI music production process could be packaged as a service. Who would hire me for this? What exactly would I be selling?"

---

## Practice Project

Pick one completed project (Blackwood Manor, Smart House Hauntings, or similar). Write a plain-English process description: what you did, in what order, and what tools you used. This becomes the foundation of a case study.
