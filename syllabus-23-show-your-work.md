# Syllabus 23: How to Show Your Work
**Status:** Learn Next — Unlocks Paid Work
**Format:** One chat session per unit, self-paced

---

## What This Is

You have built real things. But most of it lives in files no one can see. This syllabus teaches you how to write about what you built so clients understand it and trust you.

---

## Unit 1 — What a Case Study Is

**Goal:** Understand what a case study does and why it works better than a resume.

**Topics:**
- A case study tells a story: problem, solution, result
- Why clients respond to stories more than credentials
- The difference between a case study and a portfolio piece

**Session starter:**
> "Explain what a case study is and why it works better than a resume for a solo consultant. Give me a simple example of a good one. Keep the language plain."

---

## Unit 2 — The Three-Part Case Study Formula

**Goal:** Learn a simple formula you can use for every project.

**Topics:**
- Part 1: The problem (what was broken or missing?)
- Part 2: What you built or did
- Part 3: The result (what changed?)
- How to write each part in 2–4 sentences

**Session starter:**
> "Teach me a simple three-part case study formula. Then walk me through writing one for an AI workflow project. Use plain language. I want something I can replicate for any project."

---

## Unit 3 — Writing Your First Case Study

**Goal:** Produce one finished case study from your real work history.

**Topics:**
- Picking the right project to start with
- What to do when you don't have measurable results
- How to write about internal or personal projects

**Session starter:**
> "Help me write my first case study. I'll describe a project I built and you help me turn it into a proper case study using the three-part formula. Ask me what you need to know."

---

## Unit 4 — Where to Put Your Case Studies

**Goal:** Know where to publish or share your work so clients actually see it.

**Topics:**
- Your website (ryanleej.com)
- GitHub README files
- LinkedIn or professional profiles
- Simple one-page PDFs for proposals

**Session starter:**
> "Help me figure out where to publish my case studies so potential clients see them. What format works best on a personal website? On GitHub? In a proposal email?"

---

## Unit 5 — Talking About Your Work Out Loud

**Goal:** Be able to describe a project verbally in 60 seconds.

**Topics:**
- The elevator pitch format: problem, solution, result
- Practice saying it simply without technical jargon
- How to adjust based on who you're talking to

**Session starter:**
> "Help me practice talking about my work out loud. Pick one of my projects and help me build a 60-second verbal description I could say in a meeting or networking conversation."

---

## Practice Project

Pick one project from your history. Write a case study using the three-part formula. Keep it under 200 words. Plain language only.
